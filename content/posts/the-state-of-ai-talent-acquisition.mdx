<section style={{paddingBottom: "40px", borderBottom: "1px solid #e8e8e4", marginBottom: "40px"}}>
  <div style={{fontSize: "11px", letterSpacing: "0.2em", textTransform: "uppercase", color: "#999", marginBottom: "8px"}}>The Headline</div>

  <p style={{color: "#2c2c2c", marginBottom: "22px", lineHeight: 1.75}}>For decades, the TA function has been defined by its processes: requisition management, candidate sourcing, interview coordination, and offer administration. Artificial intelligence is rewriting that definition.</p>

  <div style={{background: "#0d0d0d", padding: "48px 44px", margin: "40px 0", borderRadius: "2px"}}>
    <p style={{fontSize: "24px", fontWeight: 600, lineHeight: 1.45, color: "#c8c8c8", margin: 0, letterSpacing: "-0.01em"}}>For the first time, the <strong style={{color: "#ffffff"}}>Transactional Core of TA</strong> — the work that has historically consumed the majority of practitioner time — is automatable. What remains is the work that was always most valuable and most neglected: <strong style={{color: "#ffffff"}}>Judgment, Pattern Recognition, Organizational Insight, and Human Discernment.</strong></p>
  </div>

  <p style={{color: "#2c2c2c", marginBottom: "22px", lineHeight: 1.75}}>TA leaders have dipped their toes into AI, but organizational friction within compliance and a lack of fully clear ROI are keeping most teams at the assistant stage.</p>
</section>

<section style={{paddingBottom: "40px", borderBottom: "1px solid #e8e8e4", marginBottom: "40px"}}>
  <div style={{fontSize: "11px", letterSpacing: "0.2em", textTransform: "uppercase", color: "#999", marginBottom: "8px"}}>01</div>
  <h2 style={{fontSize: "26px", fontWeight: 700, letterSpacing: "-0.02em", lineHeight: 1.25, color: "#0d0d0d", marginBottom: "28px"}}>The ATS Transition: From Action to Record</h2>

  <div style={{background: "#f0f0ec", borderLeft: "4px solid #0d0d0d", padding: "20px 24px", margin: "32px 0", borderRadius: "0 2px 2px 0"}}>
    <p style={{fontSize: "15px", fontWeight: 600, letterSpacing: "0.04em", textTransform: "uppercase", color: "#0d0d0d", margin: 0}}>65% of leaders say their stack is "mostly adequate"</p>
  </div>

  <p style={{color: "#2c2c2c", marginBottom: "22px", lineHeight: 1.75}}>The ATS works, but it wasn't built for the hiring environment organizations are operating in today. 22% say it's limited in key areas. "Adequate" means it processes requisitions, tracks candidates, and moves approvals. It does the job it was designed to do.</p>
  <p style={{color: "#2c2c2c", marginBottom: "22px", lineHeight: 1.75}}>Leaders know their ATS has limitations. They are weighing those limitations against the organizational cost of change, and for now, adequacy wins.</p>
  <p style={{color: "#2c2c2c", marginBottom: "22px", lineHeight: 1.75}}>As AI assumes responsibility for the functions the ATS was built to orchestrate, such as sourcing, screening, and scheduling, the ATS transitions from a system of action to a system of record. Leaders aren't calling their ATS broken because it isn't. It may not need to be replaced: it may just become irrelevant if it is your only standalone technology.</p>

  <h2 style={{fontSize: "26px", fontWeight: 700, letterSpacing: "-0.02em", lineHeight: 1.25, color: "#0d0d0d", marginBottom: "28px"}}>AI Adoption Is Real</h2>

  <p style={{color: "#2c2c2c", marginBottom: "22px", lineHeight: 1.75}}>Nearly all respondents have adopted some form of AI, but almost entirely at the lowest tier: writing assistance, scheduling support, and summarization. This is AI as productivity enhancement: valuable but structurally conservative. It does not change how TA operates; it makes existing operations slightly more efficient.</p>
  <p style={{color: "#2c2c2c", marginBottom: "22px", lineHeight: 1.75}}>A smaller group of TA Leaders (~25%) have crossed into task automation and agentic automation. This includes AI handling screening, sourcing, or routing with minimal human intervention, and performing sequences of tasks autonomously.</p>
  <p style={{color: "#2c2c2c", marginBottom: "22px", lineHeight: 1.75}}>For now, AI adoption in TA is not driven by company size or industry. It is driven by organizational risk tolerance.</p>

  <div style={{fontSize: "11px", letterSpacing: "0.2em", textTransform: "uppercase", color: "#999", marginBottom: "8px"}}>03</div>
  <h2 style={{fontSize: "26px", fontWeight: 700, letterSpacing: "-0.02em", lineHeight: 1.25, color: "#0d0d0d", marginBottom: "28px"}}>The Compliance Brake: Why Data Privacy is the Real Bottleneck</h2>

  <div style={{background: "#f0f0ec", borderLeft: "4px solid #0d0d0d", padding: "20px 24px", margin: "32px 0", borderRadius: "0 2px 2px 0"}}>
    <p style={{fontSize: "15px", fontWeight: 600, letterSpacing: "0.04em", textTransform: "uppercase", color: "#0d0d0d", margin: 0}}>65% cite security, privacy, or compliance concerns as their primary barrier</p>
  </div>

  <p style={{color: "#2c2c2c", marginBottom: "22px", lineHeight: 1.75}}>The top barriers to AI adoption in TA aren't coming from a lack of will or a failure to understand the advantages. Leaders know what AI can do. They want to move faster. Their own organizations are pumping the brakes, and for sound reasons.</p>
  <p style={{color: "#2c2c2c", marginBottom: "22px", lineHeight: 1.75}}>Talent Acquisition processes some of the most personally sensitive organizational data in existence: names, contact information, employment histories, and compensation data. The introduction of AI into this data environment raises questions that most enterprise legal and privacy functions are still working through.</p>
  <p style={{color: "#2c2c2c", marginBottom: "22px", lineHeight: 1.75}}>55% additionally cite IT and procurement restrictions, not as obstacles thrown up by difficult colleagues, but as the natural output of organizations that were never designed to govern technology that moves this fast.</p>

  <h2 style={{fontSize: "26px", fontWeight: 700, letterSpacing: "-0.02em", lineHeight: 1.25, color: "#0d0d0d", marginBottom: "28px"}}>AI Will Shrink TA Teams — But Leaders Are Split</h2>

  <div style={{borderLeft: "3px solid #0d0d0d", padding: "6px 0 6px 24px", margin: "36px 0"}}>
    <p style={{fontSize: "17px", fontStyle: "italic", color: "#444", margin: 0}}>No consensus on headcount impact, except one: nobody expects growth.</p>
  </div>

  <p style={{color: "#2c2c2c", marginBottom: "22px", lineHeight: 1.75}}>Leaders already running lean have less to protect and clearer visibility into what AI is actively absorbing. The leaders forecasting moderate to significant reduction (~40%) share a notable characteristic: they are predominantly operating in properly staffed, efficiently run functions. These are most commonly at early-stage and growth-stage companies where the leader has direct visibility into which tasks AI is replacing. They are observing an ongoing process and predicting its conclusion.</p>

  <h2 style={{fontSize: "26px", fontWeight: 700, letterSpacing: "-0.02em", lineHeight: 1.25, color: "#0d0d0d", marginBottom: "28px"}}>The New TA Skillset: Redefining Value</h2>

  <p style={{color: "#2c2c2c", marginBottom: "22px", lineHeight: 1.75}}>Leaders see TA evolving in multiple directions at once. The middle layer of transactional coordination is disappearing.</p>

  <div style={{display: "grid", gridTemplateColumns: "repeat(3, 1fr)", gap: "16px", margin: "36px 0"}}>

      <p style={{fontSize: "13px", color: "#999", margin: 0, lineHeight: 1.55}}>Understanding how talent decisions compound over time into organizational outcomes</p>
    </div>
    <div style={{background: "#0d0d0d", color: "#ffffff", padding: "24px 20px", borderRadius: "2px"}}>
      <div style={{fontSize: "11px", letterSpacing: "0.2em", textTransform: "uppercase", color: "#666", marginBottom: "10px"}}>02</div>
      <h4 style={{fontSize: "14px", fontWeight: 700, color: "#ffffff", marginBottom: "8px", lineHeight: 1.3}}>Org Effectiveness</h4>
      <p style={{fontSize: "13px", color: "#999", margin: 0, lineHeight: 1.55}}>Knowing where a team is strong, where it's fragile, and what kind of hire will accelerate or destabilize it</p>
    </div>
    <div style={{background: "#0d0d0d", color: "#ffffff", padding: "24px 20px", borderRadius: "2px"}}>

      <h4 style={{fontSize: "14px", fontWeight: 700, color: "#ffffff", marginBottom: "8px", lineHeight: 1.3}}>Human Assessment Depth</h4>
      <p style={{fontSize: "13px", color: "#999", margin: 0, lineHeight: 1.55}}>Moving beyond skills to understand cognitive patterns, working style, and capacity for growth</p>
    </div>
  </div>

  <p style={{fontSize: "17px", fontWeight: 600, color: "#0d0d0d", marginBottom: "8px", lineHeight: 1.75}}>75% believe TA will become more strategic and consultative.</p>
  <p style={{fontSize: "17px", fontWeight: 600, color: "#0d0d0d", marginBottom: "22px", lineHeight: 1.75}}>65% believe it will become more technical.</p>

  <p style={{color: "#2c2c2c", marginBottom: "22px", lineHeight: 1.75}}>AI will generate an unprecedented volume of data. It creates a signal environment so rich that it requires interpretive expertise to navigate. The TA leader of the next decade will need to be an organizational intelligence practitioner: a professional whose primary contribution is the capacity to make sense of complex human data and translate it into hiring decisions.</p>

  <div style={{fontSize: "11px", letterSpacing: "0.2em", textTransform: "uppercase", color: "#999", marginBottom: "8px"}}>Implications</div>
  <h2 style={{fontSize: "26px", fontWeight: 700, letterSpacing: "-0.02em", lineHeight: 1.25, color: "#0d0d0d", marginBottom: "28px"}}>What This Means for You Now</h2>

  <p style={{color: "#2c2c2c", marginBottom: "22px", lineHeight: 1.75}}>TA leaders who will define the next era are the ones who learn to own what AI produces. When agentic systems handle the transactional work, the practitioner's entire value proposition shifts to interpretation.</p>

  <h2 style={{fontSize: "26px", fontWeight: 700, letterSpacing: "-0.02em", lineHeight: 1.25, color: "#0d0d0d", marginTop: "48px", marginBottom: "28px"}}>The Trap of Sameness</h2>

  <p style={{color: "#2c2c2c", marginBottom: "22px", lineHeight: 1.75}}>There is a risk embedded in this moment. When the majority of the market adopts the same tools and applies the same logic, the competitive advantage disappears. Everyone ends up surfacing the same candidates. The differentiation won't come from having AI: it will come from the judgment you bring to what it produces.</p>
</section>

  <h2 style={{fontSize: "26px", fontWeight: 700, letterSpacing: "-0.02em", lineHeight: 1.25, color: "#0d0d0d", marginBottom: "28px"}}>Organizational Learning Velocity</h2>

  <p style={{color: "#2c2c2c", marginBottom: "22px", lineHeight: 1.75}}>The State of AI in Talent Acquisition in 2026 is a story about organizational learning velocity. The destination is a function that is analytically sophisticated and organizationally more influential.</p>
