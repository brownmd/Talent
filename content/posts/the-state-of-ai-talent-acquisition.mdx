  <div style={{fontSize: "11px", letterSpacing: "0.2em", textTransform: "uppercase", color: "var(--fg-subtle)", marginBottom: "8px"}}>The Headline</div>

  <div style={{color: "var(--fg-muted)", marginBottom: "22px", lineHeight: 1.75}}>Talent Acquisition is now fighting AI on two fronts at once, trying to adopt it internally while defending against it in the candidate pool. This survey captures how leaders are navigating that: what they're building, what's blocking them, and what they think comes next.</div>

  <div style={{color: "var(--fg-muted)", marginBottom: "22px", lineHeight: 1.75}}>The TA function has been defined by its processes: requisition management, candidate sourcing, interview coordination, and offer administration. Artificial intelligence is rewriting that definition.</div>

  <div style={{background: "#0d0d0d", padding: "40px 36px", margin: "32px 0", borderRadius: "8px"}}>
    <div style={{fontSize: "22px", fontWeight: 600, lineHeight: 1.5, color: "#c8c8c8", letterSpacing: "-0.01em"}}>For the first time, the <strong style={{color: "#ffffff"}}>Transactional Core of TA</strong>, the work that has historically consumed the majority of practitioner time, is automatable. What remains is the work that was always most valuable and most neglected: <strong style={{color: "#ffffff"}}>Judgment, Pattern Recognition, Organizational Insight, and Human Discernment.</strong></div>
  </div>

  <div style={{color: "var(--fg-muted)", marginBottom: "22px", lineHeight: 1.75}}>TA leaders have dipped their toes into AI, but organizational compliance concerns and a lack of fully clear ROI are keeping most teams at the assistant stage.</div>
</div>

<div style={{paddingBottom: "40px", borderBottom: "1px solid var(--border)", marginBottom: "40px"}}>
  <div style={{fontSize: "11px", letterSpacing: "0.2em", textTransform: "uppercase", color: "var(--fg-subtle)", marginBottom: "8px"}}>01</div>
  <h2 style={{fontSize: "24px", fontWeight: 700, letterSpacing: "-0.02em", lineHeight: 1.25, color: "var(--fg)", marginBottom: "24px"}}>The ATS Transition: From Action to Record</h2>

  <div style={{borderLeft: "4px solid var(--fg)", backgroundColor: "var(--separator)", padding: "1rem 1.25rem", borderRadius: "0 8px 8px 0", margin: "28px 0"}}>
    <div style={{fontSize: "0.9rem", fontWeight: 600, letterSpacing: "0.04em", textTransform: "uppercase", color: "var(--fg)"}}>65% of leaders say their stack is "mostly adequate"</div>
  </div>

  <div style={{color: "var(--fg-muted)", marginBottom: "22px", lineHeight: 1.75}}>The ATS works, but it wasn't built for todays hiring environment, mass applicants, fake AI candidates, and a plethora of new extensions arriving weekly. 22% say it's limited in key areas. "Adequate" means it's better than excel, it processes requisitions, tracks candidates, and moves approvals. Unless of course you are lucky enough to have a non freebie add on module ATS system.</div>
  <div style={{color: "var(--fg-muted)", marginBottom: "22px", lineHeight: 1.75}}>Leaders know their ATS has limitations. They are weighing those limitations against the organizational cost of change, and for now, adequacy wins.</div>
  <div style={{color: "var(--fg-muted)", marginBottom: "22px", lineHeight: 1.75}}>As AI assumes responsibility for the functions the ATS was built to orchestrate, such as sourcing, screening, and scheduling, the ATS transitions from a system of action to a system of record. Leaders aren't calling their ATS broken because it isn't. It may not need to be replaced: it may just become irrelevant if it is your only standalone technology.</div>
</div>

<div style={{paddingBottom: "40px", borderBottom: "1px solid var(--border)", marginBottom: "40px"}}>
  <div style={{fontSize: "11px", letterSpacing: "0.2em", textTransform: "uppercase", color: "var(--fg-subtle)", marginBottom: "8px"}}>02</div>
  <h2 style={{fontSize: "24px", fontWeight: 700, letterSpacing: "-0.02em", lineHeight: 1.25, color: "var(--fg)", marginBottom: "24px"}}>AI Adoption Is Real</h2>

  <div style={{color: "var(--fg-muted)", marginBottom: "22px", lineHeight: 1.75}}>Nearly all respondents have adopted some form of AI, but almost entirely at the lowest tier: writing assistance, scheduling support, and summarization. This is AI as productivity enhancement: valuable but structurally conservative. It does not change how TA operates; it makes existing operations slightly more efficient.</div>
  <div style={{color: "var(--fg-muted)", marginBottom: "22px", lineHeight: 1.75}}>A smaller group of TA Leaders (~25%) have crossed into task automation and agentic automation. This includes AI handling screening, sourcing, or routing with minimal human intervention, and performing sequences of tasks autonomously.</div>

  <div style={{backgroundColor: "var(--separator)", padding: "0.75rem 1rem", borderRadius: "8px", margin: "24px 0", border: "1px solid var(--border)"}}>
    <div style={{color: "var(--fg-muted)", fontSize: "0.95rem", lineHeight: 1.7}}>For now, AI adoption interestingly in TA is not driven by company size or industry. It is driven by <strong style={{color: "var(--fg)"}}>organizational risk tolerance.</strong></div>
  </div>

  <h2 style={{fontSize: "24px", fontWeight: 700, letterSpacing: "-0.02em", lineHeight: 1.25, color: "var(--fg)", marginBottom: "24px"}}>The Compliance Brake: Why Data Privacy is the Real Bottleneck</h2>

  <div style={{borderLeft: "4px solid var(--fg)", backgroundColor: "var(--separator)", padding: "1rem 1.25rem", borderRadius: "0 8px 8px 0", margin: "28px 0"}}>
    <div style={{fontSize: "0.9rem", fontWeight: 600, letterSpacing: "0.04em", textTransform: "uppercase", color: "var(--fg)"}}>65% cite security, privacy, or compliance concerns as their primary barrier</div>
  </div>

  <div style={{color: "var(--fg-muted)", marginBottom: "22px", lineHeight: 1.75}}>Leaders know what the technology can do. They want to move faster with better insights, but organizations are pumping the brakes, and for sound reasons.</div>
  <div style={{color: "var(--fg-muted)", marginBottom: "22px", lineHeight: 1.75}}>Talent Acquisition processes highly sensitive candidate data: names, contact information, interview feedback, employment histories, and compensation data. The introduction of AI into this data environment raises questions that most enterprise legal and privacy functions are still working through.</div>
  <div style={{color: "var(--fg-muted)", marginBottom: "22px", lineHeight: 1.75}}>55% additionally cite IT and procurement restrictions, not as obstacles thrown up by difficult colleagues, but possibly as the natural output of organizations that were never designed to govern technology that moves this fast.</div>

  <h2 style={{fontSize: "24px", fontWeight: 700, letterSpacing: "-0.02em", lineHeight: 1.25, color: "var(--fg)", marginBottom: "24px"}}>AI Will Shrink TA Teams, But Leaders Are Split</h2>

  <div style={{borderLeft: "3px solid var(--fg-muted)", padding: "6px 0 6px 24px", margin: "28px 0"}}>
    <div style={{fontSize: "17px", fontStyle: "italic", color: "var(--fg-muted)"}}>No consensus on headcount impact, except one: nobody expects growth.</div>
  </div>

  <div style={{color: "var(--fg-muted)", marginBottom: "22px", lineHeight: 1.75}}>Leaders already running lean have less to protect and clearer visibility into what AI is actively absorbing. The leaders forecasting moderate to significant reduction (~40%) share a notable characteristic: they are predominantly operating in properly staffed and technology forward TA teams. These are most commonly at early stage and growth stage companies enabled by un-bloated tech with fresh new modern tech environments, with direct visibility into which tasks AI is replacing.</div>

  <h2 style={{fontSize: "24px", fontWeight: 700, letterSpacing: "-0.02em", lineHeight: 1.25, color: "var(--fg)", marginBottom: "24px"}}>The New TA Skillset: Redefining Value in the AI Era</h2>

  <div style={{color: "var(--fg-muted)", marginBottom: "22px", lineHeight: 1.75}}>Leaders see TA evolving in multiple directions at once. The middle layer of recruiting, is predicted to disappear - eerily similar to USA economics.</div>

  <div style={{display: "grid", gridTemplateColumns: "repeat(3, 1fr)", gap: "16px", margin: "28px 0"}}>
    <div style={{backgroundColor: "var(--separator)", padding: "24px 20px", borderRadius: "8px", border: "1px solid var(--border)"}}>
      <div style={{fontSize: "11px", letterSpacing: "0.2em", textTransform: "uppercase", color: "var(--fg-subtle)", marginBottom: "10px"}}>01</div>
      <h4 style={{fontSize: "14px", fontWeight: 700, color: "var(--fg)", marginBottom: "8px", lineHeight: 1.3}}>People Data Fluency</h4>
      <div style={{fontSize: "13px", color: "var(--fg-muted)", lineHeight: 1.55}}>TA needs to think beyond filling seats and understand how their hiring decisions affect organizational health, and have data to influence workforce decisions.</div>
    </div>
    <div style={{backgroundColor: "var(--separator)", padding: "24px 20px", borderRadius: "8px", border: "1px solid var(--border)"}}>
      <div style={{fontSize: "11px", letterSpacing: "0.2em", textTransform: "uppercase", color: "var(--fg-subtle)", marginBottom: "10px"}}>02</div>
      <h4 style={{fontSize: "14px", fontWeight: 700, color: "var(--fg)", marginBottom: "8px", lineHeight: 1.3}}>Org Effectiveness</h4>
      <div style={{fontSize: "13px", color: "var(--fg-muted)", lineHeight: 1.55}}>Knowing where a team is strong, where it's fragile, and what kind of hire will accelerate or destabilize it</div>
    </div>
    <div style={{backgroundColor: "var(--separator)", padding: "24px 20px", borderRadius: "8px", border: "1px solid var(--border)"}}>
      <div style={{fontSize: "11px", letterSpacing: "0.2em", textTransform: "uppercase", color: "var(--fg-subtle)", marginBottom: "10px"}}>03</div>
      <h4 style={{fontSize: "14px", fontWeight: 700, color: "var(--fg)", marginBottom: "8px", lineHeight: 1.3}}>Human Assessment Depth</h4>
      <div style={{fontSize: "13px", color: "var(--fg-muted)", lineHeight: 1.55}}>Moving beyond skills to understand cognitive patterns, working style, and capacity for growth</div>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div style={{display: "flex", gap: "0.75rem", flexWrap: "wrap", margin: "24px 0"}}>
    <span style={{backgroundColor: "var(--separator)", color: "var(--fg)", padding: "0.5rem 1rem", borderRadius: "9999px", fontSize: "0.875rem", fontWeight: 600, border: "1px solid var(--border)"}}>75% more strategic &amp; consultative</span>
    <span style={{backgroundColor: "var(--separator)", color: "var(--fg)", padding: "0.5rem 1rem", borderRadius: "9999px", fontSize: "0.875rem", fontWeight: 600, border: "1px solid var(--border)"}}>65% more technical</span>
  </div>

  <div style={{color: "var(--fg-muted)", marginBottom: "22px", lineHeight: 1.75}}>AI will generate an unprecedented volume of data. It creates a signal environment so rich that it requires interpretive expertise to navigate. The TA leader of the next decade will need to be an organizational intelligence practitioner: a professional whose primary contribution is the capacity to make sense of complex human data and translate it into hiring decisions, assuming we are still talking about humans and not agentic humanoid robotic workers.</div>

  <h2 style={{fontSize: "24px", fontWeight: 700, letterSpacing: "-0.02em", lineHeight: 1.25, color: "var(--fg)", marginBottom: "24px"}}>What Leaders Are Actually Focused On</h2>

  <div style={{color: "var(--fg-muted)", marginBottom: "22px", lineHeight: 1.75}}>Talent Acquisition leaders are trying to do everything at once. AI's arrival is cracking the TA workflow. TA teams require more from technology to withstand the massive applicant volume. Leaders are justifying tech investments and enhancements from tight budgets and overworked tech teams, while trying to justify investment in systems too unreliable to produce clean numbers. Meanwhile the outside world did not wait for TA to get its house in order. Candidates are gaming the job application with autonomous submissions, AI generated resumes, AI video and more. TA is absorbing AI slop worse than a LinkedIn news feed.</div>

  <ul style={{listStyle: "none", margin: "20px 0", padding: 0}}>
    <li style={{display: "flex", alignItems: "baseline", gap: "14px", padding: "16px 0", borderBottom: "1px solid var(--border)", fontSize: "17px", color: "var(--fg-muted)"}}>
      <span style={{fontSize: "22px", fontWeight: 700, color: "var(--fg)", minWidth: "60px", lineHeight: 1}}>~65%</span>
      are prioritizing AI tools and automation: an active implementation effort.
    </li>
    <li style={{display: "flex", alignItems: "baseline", gap: "14px", padding: "16px 0", borderBottom: "1px solid var(--border)", fontSize: "17px", color: "var(--fg-muted)"}}>
      <span style={{fontSize: "22px", fontWeight: 700, color: "var(--fg)", minWidth: "60px", lineHeight: 1}}>~55%</span>
      are focused on technology consolidation: rationalizing a stack that has grown too large.
    </li>
    <li style={{display: "flex", alignItems: "baseline", gap: "14px", padding: "16px 0", fontSize: "17px", color: "var(--fg-muted)"}}>
      <span style={{fontSize: "22px", fontWeight: 700, color: "var(--fg)", minWidth: "60px", lineHeight: 1}}>~45%</span>
      are prioritizing process redesign: recognizing that AI doesn't fix broken workflows.
    </li>
  </ul>

  <div style={{color: "var(--fg-muted)", marginBottom: "22px", lineHeight: 1.75}}>These aren't three separate priorities. They're the same problem showing up three different ways.</div>

  <div style={{fontSize: "11px", letterSpacing: "0.2em", textTransform: "uppercase", color: "var(--fg-subtle)", marginBottom: "8px"}}>Implications</div>
  <h2 style={{fontSize: "24px", fontWeight: 700, letterSpacing: "-0.02em", lineHeight: 1.25, color: "var(--fg)", marginBottom: "24px"}}>What This Means for You Now</h2>

  <div style={{color: "var(--fg-muted)", marginBottom: "22px", lineHeight: 1.75}}>TA leaders who will define the next era are the ones who learn to own what AI produces: a signal environment richer than anything the function has previously had access to. When agentic systems handle the transactional work, the practitioner's entire value proposition shifts to interpretation: the capacity to synthesize hiring data, organizational dynamics, and human assessment into a recommendation made with conviction. The pitch moves from "here's the opportunity" to something more precise: "here's why this team, at this moment, needs exactly what you bring."</div>

  <div style={{color: "var(--fg-muted)", marginBottom: "22px", lineHeight: 1.75}}>When the majority of the market adopts the same tools, applies the same logic, and optimizes for the same signals, the competitive advantage those tools were supposed to create disappears. The differentiation won't come from having AI. It will come from how intelligently you use it.</div>

  <div style={{color: "var(--fg-muted)", marginBottom: "32px", lineHeight: 1.75}}>How Talent Acquisition is expected to bring value is changing. Talent Interpreter.</div>

  <div style={{background: "#0d0d0d", padding: "40px 36px", margin: "32px 0", borderRadius: "8px"}}>
    <div style={{fontSize: "22px", fontWeight: 700, lineHeight: 1.5, color: "#c8c8c8", letterSpacing: "-0.01em"}}>The TA function in the next 5 years isn't a team of only recruiters who use AI. It's an <strong style={{color: "#ffffff"}}>organizational intelligence practitioner</strong> who happens to own the hiring process.</div>
