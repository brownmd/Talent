**A note on what this data is - and isn't:**
This report does not attempt to count every AI-literate person on earth. It tracks something more specific and more valuable: *users who were caught doing AI work in public.* We analyzed 5.81 million monthly activity records drawn from GitHub between January 2021 and March 2026, identifying 2.58 million unique users who demonstrably engaged with AI tooling. This is behavioral evidence - not a survey, not a skill tag, not a self-report.

Of those 2.58 million users, location data breaks down as follows:
- **349,000** provided any location string on their GitHub profile
- **322,868** of those were resolved to at least a country-level location
- **235,054** were resolved to a specific city - the subset used for all city and metro analysis in this report

That means every city-level figure in this report is drawn from 235,054 users - roughly **9.1% of the total dataset** - and represents a self-selected, more publicly visible slice of a much larger global population. The true number of AI-literate users worldwide is almost certainly an order of magnitude larger. What we can claim is the quality of the signal, not the completeness of the census.

One further constraint worth naming upfront: the average user in this dataset was active for only **2.25 months** out of 64. The 5.81 million monthly records spread across 2.58 million users means most people appear briefly - often for short project cycles or narrow contribution bursts - rather than sustaining year-over-year engagement. A much smaller core is active consistently. That persistent cohort is where the deepest signal lives.

## What This Article Is About: Mapping AI Talent Density

This article is a practical map of where publicly visible AI talent is concentrated, where density is rising, and how quickly those patterns are shifting.

The headline takeaway is straightforward: AI has moved from a niche capability to a mainstream workforce expectation, and the adoption curve in this dataset reflects that shift.

### Executive Summary

- AI adoption has grown from early-specialist activity into sustained mainstream workforce behavior.
- The strongest density remains in established hubs (for example, Bay Area, Seattle, Boston), but secondary metros are accelerating.
- US visible AI participation in this dataset grew roughly 6x from 2021 to 2026, signaling a structural shift rather than a one-time spike.
- For TA/TI teams, the practical question is not whether adoption is happening, but where concentration and momentum are strongest.
- Use this report as a directional market map: prioritize metro strategy by combining scale (density) with acceleration (growth rate).

## Part III: Growth Hotspots - Where Momentum Is Building

### Reading the Growth Signal

The most durable growth signal is the cumulative expansion of the AI-literate user pool across the 2021-2026 period. Looking at US-located users specifically:

- **January 2021:** 507 US users with verified location observed in that month
- **March 2023:** 2,922 US-located users observed in that month
- **January 2025:** 6,417 US-located users observed in that month
- **March 2026 (latest):** 3,133 US-located users observed in that month

The US-located monthly cohort has grown roughly **6x from 2021 to 2026**, closely tracking the global growth curve - suggesting the US is keeping pace with, rather than driving, global AI user expansion.

Given the broader adoption of AI tooling across functions, this continued growth is not surprising; what matters strategically is where that growth is densest and where it is accelerating fastest.

### What This Means for Talent Sourcing

The practical implication for talent buyers: the addressable pool of demonstrably AI-literate users has not just grown - it has **structurally shifted** in composition. In 2021, AI-literate GitHub users were a small specialist group averaging 97-125 in AI literacy score. By 2025, the pool had expanded to include a much broader range of engagement depths, from highly specialized researchers (scores in the thousands) to more recent adopters building on top of existing models and APIs.

The deepest talent - users with sustained, high-intensity engagement over multiple years - remains concentrated in the established tech hubs. But the widest geographic distribution of AI literacy is expanding: cities like Nashville, Pittsburgh, Raleigh, Orlando, and Columbus are building the foundations of AI user communities that did not exist at measurable scale in 2021.

## Part IV: The Leaderboards - Who Shows the Highest Measured AI GitHub Interaction?

### Understanding the Scoring Model

AI literacy score is a cumulative, behavioral metric: it accrues for each month a user has observed interaction in AI-related repositories, weighted by contribution type (commits > PRs > issues > stars) and repository quality tier. A user with 21 months of high-intensity engagement on frontier model repositories will significantly outscore a user with the same total months of light activity. The leaderboards below reflect all-time cumulative score across January 2021 - March 2026.

Score is not a rank of "best developer." It is a rank of *sustained, measurable AI engagement* on public GitHub - a behavioral signal of how deeply and how consistently someone has been doing AI work in public.

## Closing: What 2.58 Million Verified Users Represents

The headline number is 2.58 million. It needs context.

**What it is:** The largest behavioral dataset of verified AI-literate users assembled from public GitHub activity. Every user in this set was caught doing AI work - not surveyed, not tagged with a skill, not inferred from job title. They committed code, opened issues, starred repositories, or forked tools in AI-related contexts, and we recorded it.

**What it isn't:** A count of all AI-literate users. The true population - including users at companies with private GitHub organizations, users using non-GitHub tools, non-coding AI practitioners, enterprise ML engineers, and the vast global workforce building on AI APIs - is almost certainly 10x-50x larger. This dataset captures the publicly visible surface of a much deeper phenomenon.

**The honest framing:** We identified 2.58 million users who left a verifiable AI footprint on the world's largest code collaboration platform across a 64-month period. They represent a conservative floor - the provably AI-literate, publicly verifiable cohort - inside a global AI talent population that is impossible to fully enumerate but clearly expanding.

The cumulative growth curve is the key signal. From 16,323 total users identified in January 2021, to 502,049 by March 2023, to 1.74 million by February 2025, to 2.58 million by March 2026, the dataset shows large-scale expansion in publicly verifiable AI activity.
