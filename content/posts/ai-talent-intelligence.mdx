# GitHub AI Activity Surged 158x (2021-2026)
### A Global Talent Intelligence Report Tracking 2.58 Million Verified Users Across 64 Months

https://github.com/brownmd/AI_Talent_Intelligence
---

## Executive Snapshot

- The observable AI talent market expanded from 16,323 users (Jan 2021) to 2,579,030 users (Mar 2026), a **158x** increase.
- US concentration is still strongest in established hubs, but growth momentum is broadening into mid-tier metros.
- The practical use case is market prioritization: where to source now, where to build pipeline next, and where to expect rising competition.

## Scope and Confidence

This dataset tracks **public, behavioral AI activity** on GitHub, not self-reported skills.

- 5.81M monthly records (Jan 2021-Mar 2026)
- 2.58M unique users with observed AI-related interactions
- 349,000 users with any location string
- 322,868 resolved to country level
- 235,054 resolved to city level (used for city/metro analysis)

Interpretation boundary:

- City/metro findings are strong for **relative concentration and momentum**.
- They are not a full census of all AI-capable workers.
- Average observed activity is 2.25 months per user, indicating a broad signal with a smaller deep-engagement core.

## Closing: What 2.58 Million Verified Users Represents

2.58 million needs context.

What it is: a large, behavior-based dataset of publicly verifiable AI engagement on GitHub across 64 months.

What it is not: a full count of all AI-literate workers.

The decision-grade signal is trajectory: 16,323 (Jan 2021) to 502,049 (Mar 2023) to 1.74M (Feb 2025) to 2.58M (Mar 2026). AI literacy has moved into mainstream.

As workforce data becomes more widely available, talent acquisition must evolve from data collection to data interpretation. The differentiator is no longer who has data, but who can synthesize external market signals and internal hiring outcomes into clear signals. TA's strategic role is to translate that evidence into better capability planning, smarter hiring choices, and stronger long-term workforce readiness.
